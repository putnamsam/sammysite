Chapter Two · The Plan

We don't need magic.
We need consistency.

There's no risky bet here, no lottery ticket. Just five simple habits repeated for ten years — and the quiet power of compound growth doing the rest.

First — right now
Clear every debt
The credit cards and RV loan — about $53,000 — go first. High-interest debt works against us every single day. Once it's gone, that money starts working for us instead.
Every year · 2026–2036
Fill every tax-advantaged bucket
Both 401ks maxed at $23,500 each. Our shared HSA maxed at $8,750 (with $1,000 catch-ups once we each turn 55). That's over $55,000 a year going in before taxes — and Washington takes no state income tax, so every dollar stretches further.
Every year · 2027–2036
Turn Sam's Disney stock into freedom
Sam earns around $73,000 a year in Disney shares. We sell them the moment they vest and invest the proceeds — about $55,000 a year after tax — into simple index funds. This becomes the bridge that carries us through our early retirement years.
Every year · 2026–2035
Draw down Dad's gift, thoughtfully
We have ten years to empty the inherited IRA. We take a measured amount each year and reinvest it. By 2035 it's fully distributed — leaving us a clean, simple tax picture heading into retirement.
Before we retire
Own our home outright
The mortgage is gone before we stop working. No house payment in retirement — just the taxes, insurance, and upkeep. That single fact makes everything else easier.

Chapter Five · The Journey

How the money
carries us through

Retirement isn't one long chapter — it unfolds in stages. Each one draws from different accounts in the smartest order, keeping taxes low and our savings working as long as possible.

55–59½
2036–2041 · The bridge
Living on the bridge fund
We retire a few years before we can touch our 401ks without penalty. That's exactly why we built the taxable investment account — around $1.3 million. We live off it, paying low capital-gains rates instead of income tax, while our retirement accounts keep growing untouched. Our health savings covers medical costs, tax-free.
59½–70
2041–2051 · Full access
Everything unlocks
Now every account is open to us. We withdraw in the most tax-efficient order and quietly move money from our 401ks into Roth accounts during these lower-income years — paying a little tax now to avoid much more later. This is the invisible work that pays off for decades.
70+
2051+ · Guaranteed income
Social Security & Medicare
By waiting until 70, we lock in the largest possible Social Security checks — for life. Medicare replaces the health insurance we'd been buying ourselves. Our spending naturally settles down. This is the steadiest, most secure stretch of the whole journey.
73+
2054+ · Legacy
What we leave behind
The government requires us to start drawing from our 401ks at 73 — but the Roth conversions we did years earlier keep those forced withdrawals small. By now, travel has slowed and life is simpler. The focus turns to what we pass on to Noah, Sophia, and Tobin.
