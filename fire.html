How our money works
over time

Retirement isn't one thing — it evolves. Here's how we sequence our accounts to minimize taxes and maximize what we keep.

01
2036–2041 · Ages 55–59½
The bridge years
We retire 4–5 years before we can touch our 401ks penalty-free. That's why we built the taxable brokerage (~$1.3M). We live off that, pay low capital gains rates instead of income tax rates, and let the 401ks keep growing untouched. HSA covers all our medical costs tax-free.
02
2041–2051 · Ages 59½–70
Full access — smart withdrawals
Now everything is open. We withdraw in the most tax-efficient order: taxable accounts first (low capital gains rates), then carefully timed 401k withdrawals. We also start converting 401k money to Roth in low-income years — paying tax now at 12–22% to avoid higher rates later when Social Security kicks in.
03
2051+ · Age 70+
Social Security + Medicare
By waiting until 70 to claim Social Security, we maximize our guaranteed lifetime income — permanently. Medicare eliminates the ACA premium we've been paying. Our expenses naturally start declining too. This is the most financially stable phase of retirement.
04
2058+ · Age 73+
Legacy & simplification
Required distributions from our 401ks begin at 73. The Roth conversions we did in phase 2 reduce how much we're forced to take. Travel has slowed, spending is lower, and the focus shifts to what we leave behind for Noah, Sophia, and Tobin.
